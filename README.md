# Dinamic-Pricing-Analysis-in-Transportantion-Services
This project explores the relationship between operational and customer-related variables and the  **average ratings** of trips in a transportation service operating under a dynamic pricing model.

Through statistical analysis, the study evaluates whether variables such as the number of rides, expected ride duration, and customer loyalty significantly affect the average rating provided by customers.

## Dataset
The dataset contains information related to ride-hailing transportation services that operate under a dynamic pricing strategy. It consists of 1000 observations and 10 variables describing trip characteristics. 

For this analysis, a subset of variables was selected based on their potential relationship with customer satisfaction. The study focused on:

* **Average Rating** -> Dependent Variable
* **Number of Riders** -> Independent Variable
* **Expected Ride Duration** -> Independent Variable
* **Customer Loyalty** -> Independent Variable

## Methodology
* Data cleaning and preprocessing.
* Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).
* Descriptive statistical analysis.
* Correlation analysis.
* Visualization of variable distributions and relationships.
* Evaluation of the explanatory power of the independent variables on Average Rating.

## Key Findings
* The relationships between operational variables and Average Rating were analyzed using statistical techniques.
* Correlation levels were evaluated to identify potential determinants of customer satisfaction.
* Results suggest whether Average Rating is significantly associated with the studied variables or is more strongly influenced by external factors not represented in the dataset.
